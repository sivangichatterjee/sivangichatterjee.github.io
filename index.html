My current research investigates how large language models behave when they encounter errors in multi-step agentic pipelines. Beyond RLVR: Easier and Robust Post-Training via On-Policy Self-Distillation

Ongoing Research

This work explores whether large language models can improve their own reasoning without relying on an external teacher. Recent findings show that off-policy methods like SFT and off-policy distillation often lead to forgetting, while on-policy approaches better preserve existing capabilities. Building on the observation that even incorrect chain-of-thought traces from a distributionally closer source can boost reasoning performance, we study whether a model's own synthetic outputs, both correct and incorrect, are sufficient to strengthen its reasoning through self-distillation alone, removing the need for a separate teacher model entirely.
